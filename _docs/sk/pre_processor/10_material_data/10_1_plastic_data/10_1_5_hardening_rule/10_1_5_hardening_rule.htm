10.1.5. Hardening rule

Currently, two models for hardening (HDNRUL) are supported, kinematic and isotropic. For an isotropic model, as a material yields and plastically deforms, the yield surface expands uniformly or isotropically. Thus, the yield strain in all directions is the same. However, for a kinematic model, the yield surface shifts as the material yields. The kinematic hardening model is required if the Bauschinger effect is to be modeled. This is valid only for the elasto-plastic objects under small deformation.
